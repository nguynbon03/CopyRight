Dwayne Johnson's XFL Relaunch Draws Record Viewership and Star-Studded Ownership Group

The former wrestler turned mogul has assembled an ownership group including LeBron James, Serena Williams, and Jay-Z to transform the spring football league.

Robert Pham2026-07-1410 minarticle

When Dwayne Johnson purchased the XFL out of bankruptcy in 2020 for $15 million, many dismissed it as a vanity project. Six years later, the league is posting viewership numbers that rival established sports properties, and Johnson has assembled an ownership group that reads like a Forbes celebrity rich list.

The latest additions to the ownership consortium include LeBron James, Serena Williams, Jay-Z, and technology entrepreneur Jensen Huang. Together, they bring not just capital but global brand recognition that has transformed the XFL from a curiosity into a legitimate spring sports destination.

"People told me football in the spring would never work," Johnson told CelebPost from his office at XFL headquarters in Arlington, Texas. "But I knew the appetite was there. People love football. They love it so much they don't want to wait seven months between Super Bowls. We just had to give them a product worth watching."

The numbers back him up. XFL viewership has grown 340% since Johnson's takeover, with this season averaging 8.2 million viewers per game — more than Major League Soccer and approaching NHL regular-season numbers. The league's championship game drew 15 million viewers, a spring football record.

The business model has evolved significantly. The XFL has secured a $2.1 billion media rights deal with ESPN and Amazon, launched a successful merchandising line, and established partnerships with 12 NFL teams that use the league as a developmental pipeline. Johnson credits the ownership group's celebrity status with opening doors that traditional sports executives couldn't.

"When LeBron James walks into a room, people listen," Johnson says. "When Serena Williams endorses your product, consumers trust it. Our ownership group isn't just wealthy — they're the most trusted voices in sports and entertainment."
